First things first

This service is meant to help organize priorities. If you're already working with Trello, you'll find your existing cards ordered by their due date with important cards listed first. If you're not yet using Trello, you should give it a try.

"Eisenhower-Matrix"

The Eisenhower matrix is a time management principle to prioritize and plan tasks especially in situations when there's just too much to do. Another use-case of this matrix is to prioritize long-term goals for oneself.

Important and urgent tasks should be done by yourself immediately or as soon as possible. Enough time of each day should be reserved for these kind of tasks. However, the ultimate goal would be to avoid tasks becoming urgent and keeping this quadrant empty - this will allow you working on the important tasks.

Important tasks should be scheduled ahead of time or be done whenever time allows it.

Urgent but less important tasks might be delegated or resolved without spending too much time on them.

Tasks that you would consider neighter important nor urgent might be things that you should simply not do or schedule for doing later.

With First things first scheduled tasks will automatically switch into the urgent lane once they become urgent.

How to use

Drag & drop to schedule: First things first allows you to drag and drop tasks between the four quadrants to triage the tasks urgency and importance without spending too much time on planning.

Board selection: With "Cards I'm assigned to" all cards you're a member of will be displayed. (Pro tip: In Trello hover over a card and press the space-bar to assign a card to yourself, no mouse-click required.) If a specific board is being selected from the dropdown menu, all cards of the selected board will be displayed.

Urgency and importance can be updated by setting the due date and the label of cards. By default, the current day and the red label will be used to determine importance and urgency, both of which can be defined in the menu on top of the matrix.

Secure

Your Trello data will only be transmitted via HTTPS between your browser and the Trello servers and no data will be stored outside of the Trello system.

Please sign in with your Trello account first. After signing in, cards you're assigned to will be loaded into the matrix, or you can select specific boards from the list to load all cards from.

Your data will not be stored anywhere else and the only data being modified by this service is the due date and one label used to mark a card as being important.
